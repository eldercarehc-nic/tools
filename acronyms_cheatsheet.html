Acronyms & Key Terms
A comprehensive glossary of clinical roles, care levels, insurance types, software terms, and operational concepts used across Elder Care Homecare.
Caregiver Roles
HOM HomemakerNon-medical household support — cleaning, cooking, errands, companionship. No cert required. No ADLs. No nurse supervision. Can't use with LTC (usually).
PCAPatient Care Assistant. Licensed. Helps with some IADLs, no ADLs. Can be used as HOM or HHA.
HHAHome Health Aide. Certified. Helps with ADLs and IADLs. LTC Mon–Fri only. Requires RN supervision.
CNACertified Nursing Assistant. Many come from hospitals. Used as HHAs or HOMs. Helps with ADLs and IADLs.
CGCaregiver — general term for any aide providing direct care.
Nursing Roles
LPNLicensed Practical Nurse. 2-yr nursing school. Wound care, IV infusion, diabetes management. Basic meds only.
RNRegistered Nurse. 4-yr nursing school. Ventilator/tracheostomy, tube feeding, medication pre-pour/mgmt. 12-hr max shift; 24-hr = 4 days max, sleep 13 hrs / work 11 hrs.
RN CMRegistered Nurse Care Manager. Coordinates and oversees patient care — medical, social, and supportive services.
NPNurse Practitioner. Master's degree. Can issue doctor's orders (X-rays, scans, MRIs) and prescriptions.
BSNBachelor of Science in Nursing. Combines clinical training with leadership, research, and public health coursework.
Other Clinical & Allied Health
CMCare Management. Coordinated approach to supporting individuals with health, social, or functional needs.
SWSocial Worker. Helps individuals and families navigate health, relationships, and social systems.
DPSDirector of Patient Services.
OTOccupational Therapy. We outsource — CHHA typically involved.
PTPhysical Therapy. We outsource — CHHA typically involved.
SLPSpeech Language Pathologist. We outsource — CHHA typically involved.
SNVSkilled Nurse Visit. A nurse visit to the patient's home.
SNFSkilled Nursing Facility. Inpatient care setting.
Care Concepts
ADLActivities of Daily Living. Bathing, brushing teeth, dressing, toileting, transferring. Hands-on personal care.
IADLInstrumental ADLs. Laundry, groceries, cleaning. Less hands-on than ADLs.
Care PlanDocument outlining health needs, care goals, and caregiver actions.
Plan of CareWhere caregivers receive their list of duties to perform during a shift.
485Official physician document certifying patient eligibility for home health under Medicare. Doctor's orders for care.
DMEDurable Medical Equipment.
DOHDepartment of Health.
Hoyer LiftPatient transfer device. Requires two people — they tip over with one.
Insurance & Payment
LTCLong Term Care insurance. Ameritas, MetLife, John Hancock, AARP, Mutual of Omaha, Brighthouse, NY Life. Triggered when 2+ ADLs are impaired or cognitive impairment present.
MCMedicare. Federal insurance for 65+, certain disabilities, ESRD, ALS.
MAMedicaid. Joint federal/state insurance for low income or limited resources.
AOBAssignment of Benefits. Who receives LTC/insurance payment. We do not do AOB — client pays us directly.
Insurance We Do Not Accept
Medicare, Medicaid, commercial insurance (BlueCross, Aetna, United), military (Tricare, Humana). We only accept LTC insurance and private pay.
Agency Types
LHCSA UsLicensed Home Health Care Agency. Private homecare agency approved by NYS. Allows us to accept LTC insurance.
CHHA Not UsCertified Home Health Agency. Takes insurance (Medicare/Medicaid). Examples: MJHS, Girling, Visiting Nurse Service, Centerlite, Americare. Pronounced "CHA."

Priority Codes — ClearCare
Level 1 — High Priority
Must have care. Condition is highly unstable. Every possible effort must be made — even during emergencies. Examples: life-sustaining equipment, skilled wound care, unstable with no caregiver support.
Level 2 — Moderate Priority
Services may be postponed with phone contact. Condition is somewhat unstable — care should happen that day but can be safely delayed briefly.
Level 3 — Low Priority
Patient is stable with access to informal resources. Can safely miss a scheduled visit with family or personal support.

Shift Types
2 × 128am–8pm and 8pm–8am (or similar split). Aide is up and available at all times.
Live-In24-hour shift. Paid at 13-hr rate. Aide sleeps 6 hrs, may get up once or twice a night. Needs partitioned space.
HourlyMust be more than 8 hours due to aide travel time.

Performance evaluation — 1–2 times per year
Drug screen — annual
Flu vaccine — annual
Nurse Companion
A Nurse Companion is a homemaker case — the nurse is not performing nursing duties. Provides companionship only.
