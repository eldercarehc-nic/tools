NY Home Care Clinical Reference
Quick Ref · RN vs LPN · New York
Registered Nurse
RN
Broad scope of practice including complex procedures, independent assessments, comprehensive care planning, and clinical supervision of LPNs, HHAs, and PCAs.
Licensed Practical Nurse
LPN
Routine and basic care tasks performed under RN supervision. Handles standard nursing interventions, limited medication administration, and patient support and education reinforcement.
Governing Regulation
Primary regulation10 NYCRR Part 766
Key section766.5 — Clinical Supervision
Also governed byNYS Nurse Practice Act
HHA supervisorRN only
Skill & annual evalsRN only (HHAs, PCAs, LPNs)
766.5 — Clinical Supervision Rules
1
Agencies must ensure supervision includes case review, in-home visits, demonstration, evaluation, and clinical consultation.
2
Home health aides must be supervised by an RN.
3
RNs are the only discipline permitted to conduct skills and annual evaluations on HHAs, PCAs, and LPNs.
Key Takeaway
RN in Home Care: Broader responsibilities — complex care, independent decision-making, care planning, and supervision of the entire home care team.
LPN in Home Care: Important team member handling focused, routine tasks. Assists with basic care and provides support under RN direction and supervision.
RN Duties in Home Care
Assessment & Planning
Performs physical exams and comprehensive patient assessments
Develops comprehensive skilled nursing care plans
Performs triage and independent clinical decision-making
Skilled Nursing Interventions
Medication administration including advanced and IV medications
IV Therapy, Central lines (PICC/ports), TPN
Complex wound care — irrigation, VAC therapy, surgical wounds
Ostomy care, catheter care, feeding and therapeutic diets
Monitor and manage chronic conditions
Education & Support
In-depth patient and family education on medications, conditions, and home safety
Emotional support for patients and families adjusting to chronic or serious illness
Monitoring, Documentation & Supervision
Tracks vital signs, symptoms, changes — reports to physician and revises care plans
Maintains accurate, timely, and complete clinical documentation
Supervises LPNs and HHAs and conducts all skills and annual evaluations
LPN Duties in Home Care
Monitoring & Basic Care
Vital signs monitoring and basic patient assessment (no independent assessments)
Basic patient care and routine tasks under RN supervision
Medication Administration (Limited)
Oral and topical medications
G tube / PEG tube feedings and medications
Basic injections as directed by supervising RN
Nursing Interventions
Urinary straight catheterization
Tracheal suctioning
Sterile dressing changes (simple wounds only)
G tube feedings and medication administration
Education & Documentation
Reinforces education provided by RNs — does not independently develop patient education plans
Accurate charting: progress notes, medication records, and flow sheets
LPN Restrictions — Cannot:
Determine nursing diagnoses or develop nursing care plans
Administer IV medications or manage/access central lines (PICC, ports)
Perform independent nursing assessments
Provide mental health counseling or case management
